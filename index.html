DIGITAL INFRASTRUCTURE IN MALAYSIA

How Has Internet Usage Grown Across ASEAN (2016-2021)?

From 2016 to 2021, Internet penetration in Malaysia surged, exceeding 90%, placing it among the top three ASEAN nations.
Other countries like Singapore and Brunei saw similar trends, while Cambodia and Myanmar lagged behind.
The growth gap between higher and lower income nations within ASEAN became more pronounced during this period.

Source: World Bank Open Data

Source: Department of Statistics, Malaysia

Malaysia's Service Penetration Trends (2019-2023)

Mobile cellular penetration in Malaysia reached nearly 150% by 2023, reflecting its dominance in the digital services landscape, while mobile broadband grew steadily to 133.3%.

Fixed broadband grew slower, increased to 49.1%, suggesting ongoing infrastructure challenges or a preference for mobile services.

In cotrast, Pay TV penetrations fell from 86.3% to 78.5%. The decrease may indicate a shift in consumer preferences toward on-demand and online services.

How Are Malaysians Using Devices Across States?

Across Malaysian states, mobile phones are the most dominant device, with usage exceeding 90% nationwide. Tablet usage varies by state, peaking in Selangor at 37.3%.

In terms of portable computers, Selangor again stands out with usage up 49.9%, showing a higher engagement with this device.

Other portable devices are less frequently used, suggesting minimal engagement across all states. This reflects a digital divide in devide accessibility.


Source: Department of Statistics, Malaysia

Which States Lead in 5G Coverage?

Source: Malaysian Communications and Multimedia Commission (MCMC)

Up to 2024 Q2, Kuala Lumpur and Selangor lead in 5G coverage, boasting over 90% connectivity, while states in East Malaysia, such as
Sabah and Sarawak, show lower coverage rates. The disparity highlights the differences in infrastructure development and
access to advanced technology across the country, and the need for greater attention to less connected regions.

Source: OpenSignal


Who is the Best 5G Provider in Malaysia?

Digi outperforms all other providers, consistently ranking 1st in every category, while Umobile lags behind in multiple categories.

Celcom and Unifi are closing the gap, showing improvements in their overall performance. Meanwhile, Yes offers the weakest overall service quality, ranking lowest in most categories.
